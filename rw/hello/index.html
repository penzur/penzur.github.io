/rw

In the unix/linux system, each file is associated with a bunch of permission flags that will determine the access of a certain group or individual across the operating system. And the permision flag kind of look something like this. 


u   g   o   user   group date        file
rw- --- --- penzur staff May 01 2020 some-file.pdf

The first three columns deal with permissions. And under the u(user) column, the enabled flags are r(read) and w(write) and the last flag is actually x(execute) which is disabled in this case.
